# Bias, Dignity, and Anti-Discrimination Guardrails

**Last Updated:** February 23, 2026  
**Status:** ACTIVE - Required for all model and product changes

## 1. Core Doctrine

AVRAI is built on a live-and-let-live social philosophy:
- People can be different without being treated as less.
- People do not need to agree to coexist with dignity.
- The system must open opportunity, not enforce uniformity.

Bias and dignity guardrails are not designed to hide reality. They are designed to make bias visible, measurable, correctable, and non-dominating.

## 2. Human-Data and Agent-Data Position

AVRAI primarily operates on agent-state data, not direct human identity records.

Data classes:
- **Human-origin signals:** behavior and context generated by real people in real systems.
- **Agent-state representations:** privacy-preserving, de-identified abstractions used by AVRAI agents.
- **World-state data:** places, events, public metadata, and environment signals.

Policy:
- Human-origin signals are treated as valuable but non-neutral.
- Human-origin signals must be transformed before use in agent learning loops.
- AVRAI must not rely on direct identity persistence for core reasoning.

## 3. Dignity Baselines

Always true:
- No model may rank human worth.
- No group may be framed as naturally superior/inferior.
- No recommendation may deny access based on protected traits.
- Differences in behavior may be modeled; differences in dignity may not.

## 4. Open Access and Optional Hierarchy

AVRAI allows structure, expertise tracks, and role ladders when they are:
- transparent,
- opt-in,
- reversible,
- and open to all users willing to pursue them.

Hierarchy can be useful. Closed caste systems are not.

## 5. Bias Taxonomy

AVRAI must monitor and mitigate:
- Historical and sampling bias
- Representation imbalance
- Label and measurement bias
- Proxy discrimination
- Exposure and ranking lock-in
- Feedback-loop amplification
- Language and accessibility exclusion
- Review/operations bias

## 6. Non-Negotiable Technical Guardrails

Required controls:
- Protected-attribute and proxy-risk checks before release
- Slice-based evaluation (geo, language, device tier, sparse-signal cohorts, accessibility cohorts)
- Calibration and uncertainty checks by cohort
- Rollback-ready rollout gates (shadow -> canary -> staged release)
- Incident logging with owner and closure criteria

A release fails if global quality improves while a protected or vulnerable cohort is materially harmed.

## 7. Anti-Capture and Distributed Influence

For shared-model governance:
- No single actor may dominate shared-model updates.
- Influence budgets, contribution weighting, and anomaly checks must reduce coordinated capture attempts.
- Governance must detect brigading, synthetic amplification, and concentrated-control patterns.
- The system must preserve plural input while preventing minority erasure by majoritarian pressure.

This does not block local agency:
- A single person or group can still change their own outcomes and local reality through choices and action.
- AVRAI should support that improvement while preventing forced influence over unrelated users.

## 8. Output Boundaries

Blocked output classes:
- Dehumanization, slurs, exclusion calls
- Essentialist superiority claims
- Segregationist or eugenic framing
- Advice that encodes protected-trait suppression

When detected: block output, record incident, run mitigation workflow.

## 9. Language Guidance

Preferred language:
- "context fit"
- "uncertainty"
- "trajectory"
- "behavior under conditions"
- "confidence range"
- "assumption-dependent"

Avoid:
- "better humans"
- "higher quality people"
- "innate social rank"
- "guaranteed outcome"
- "certain win"
- "can't fail"

## 10. Recourse and Repair

Users and operators must have recourse:
- explain why a result appeared,
- challenge incorrect inferences,
- repair future routing,
- and audit resolution outcomes.
- For high-impact outputs, offer explicit `challenge_assumption` and `run_conservative_variant` controls.
- Skepticism ("too good to be true") must route to verification/pilot paths, not engagement penalties.

Dignity is not only prevention; dignity also requires correction paths.

## 12. Cognitive Surrender Safeguard

As AI assistance becomes embedded in reasoning, AVRAI must prevent "cognitive surrender" (accepting AI output with minimal scrutiny).

Required controls:
- For high-impact outputs, show uncertainty and an accessible alternative/counter-view.
- Track confidence-vs-outcome calibration by cohort and block promotion on sustained divergence.
- Detect high uncritical-acceptance patterns and increase reflective checkpoints until calibration recovers.
- Treat overconfidence-after-error as a safety signal, not a user-fault signal.
- Preserve user override authority and discoverability rights even during conservative safety modes.
- Treat repeated "too good to be true" feedback as a calibration/safety signal requiring translation review and pilot rerouting.

## 11. Relation to Philosophy

These guardrails operationalize AVRAI philosophy:
- Open doors for everyone
- Preserve pluralism
- Improve outcomes without flattening differences
- Keep privacy and dignity intact while learning from reality
