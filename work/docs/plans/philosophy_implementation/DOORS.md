# DOORS - Canonical Philosophy

**Last Updated:** February 20, 2026  
**Status:** Canonical Philosophy Definition

## The Doctrine

There is no secret to life. There are doors that have not yet been opened.

In AVRAI:
- A place can be a door.
- A person can be a door.
- A community can be a door.
- An event can be a door.
- A project, partnership, or learning path can be a door.

AVRAI is the key. People choose which doors to open.

## What This Rejects

- Not a "single right path" product
- Not a social scoring product
- Not a system for assigning human rank
- Not a gamified replacement for real life

## What This Requires

- Real-world utility over engagement theater
- Pluralism and open participation
- Personal agency and recourse
- Transparent model behavior and governance

## Doors and Diversity

Different people open different doors. That is expected and healthy.
AVRAI must preserve this diversity while still helping people discover new options.

## Doors and Expertise

Expert systems and role ladders are valid when they stay:
- open,
- opt-in,
- transparent,
- and challengeable.

Useful hierarchy is allowed. Human worth hierarchy is forbidden.
